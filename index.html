The Problem

Moving Beyond Inefficient Systems.

Fashion still runs on standardized sizing and repetitive sampling cycles—systems designed for mass production, not human bodies or the planet. This leads to wasted materials, unsold inventory, and a disconnect between creative intent and technical execution.

HAI Systems replaces legacy processes with a computational pipeline that starts with the individual and ends with precise, on-demand fabrication.

70% reduction in sampling waste

By simulating and validating garments in silico before a single physical sample exists.

0 unsold inventory

Design-on-Demand workflows produce only what is already spoken for.

100% personalized fit

Biometric and contextual data drive every pattern and structural decision.

The Impact

Engineering New Industry Pillars.

For Designers

Stay in full creative authorship while the system handles geometric and material complexity.

  • Iterate in parametric space without sacrificing intuition.
  • Preview physical behavior and drape before fabrication.

A circular economy model built on precision, not excess.

  • Radically reduce material waste through digital validation.
  • Localize production to shorten supply chains.
  • Design garments for repair, reuse, and material recovery.

Team & Philosophy

Engineering the Next Chapter of Fashion.

HAI Systems is led by Chelsea Hai, a computational designer working at the intersection of fashion, architecture, and advanced manufacturing.

With a background in Rhino, Grasshopper, and algorithmic design, Chelsea builds systems that treat garments as dynamic, data-rich infrastructures rather than static products.

Mission

We believe the next era of fashion will not be defined by faster consumption, but by better systems—systems that encode responsibility, intelligence, and beauty into every step of the lifecycle.

HAI Systems exists to prototype those infrastructures: tools and processes that make it possible to design fewer, better garments with precision, care, and planetary limits in mind.
